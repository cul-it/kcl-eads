Abstract:
Photographs of Knowles Loom Works and Crompton & Knowles Loom Works machinery.
Creator:
Robert Patterson
Quanitities:
1 folders
Language:
Collection material in English
Images depict several types of either the Knowles Loom Works or the Crompton & Knowles Loom Works textile machinery. These particular machines were probably built or sold in England by Hutchinson, Hollingworth & Company, Ltd. All machinery photographs are attributed to Robert Patterson, Lancashire, England, but are stamped on the back, "Castle Museum York, The Folk Museum of Yorkshire Life." Also includes five photographs of weaver's cottages at the York Castle Museum, York, England. These photographs are also stamped "Castle Museum York, The Folk Museum of Yorkshire Life" on the back.
Access to the collections in the Kheel Center is restricted. Please contact a reference archivist for access to these materials.

Scope and Contents
Gelatin silver print snapshot. Image depicts a Crompton & Knowles tappet loom (tappet is the British term for cam) in Lancashire, England, with a 4 by 1 shuttle box, and 40" reed space. Item 6: Crompton & Knowles (?) knife-loom
 1965
Format: black and white photograph
Scope and Contents
Gelatin silver print snapshot. Image depicts a knife-loom in Lancashire, England, for cut moquette weaving. Moquette can be any of several varieties of warp pile fabrics commonly made with the pile of wool or mohair and the ground of cotton; in this case the pile was cut rather than looped. Usually made in upholstery weight and construction, but sometimes applied to certain carpet styles. No maker's name visible, but possibly a Crompton & Knowles loom. A Crompton & Knowles tappet (cam) loom is on the right. The name Crompton & Knowles would place the manufacture of these looms after 1897, when the Crompton Loom Works and the Knowles Loom Works merged into Crompton & Knowles Loom Works. 15.75 x 10 cm.

Item 9: Tappet [cam] and dobby looms
 1965
Format: black and white photograph
Scope and Contents
Gelatin silver print snapshot. Image depicts several looms in Lancashire, England. One (in the right foreground) has no maker's name visible and is probably French. Box 1 Folder 1
Item 10: Tappet [cam] and dobby looms
 1965
Format: black and white photograph
Scope and Contents
Gelatin silver print snapshot. Image depicts three looms in Lancashire, England. Front to back, they are a possible Crompton & Knowles tappet (the British term for cam) loom, with a box dobby and 34" reed space; a possible Crompton & Knowles dobby loom, with 30" reed space, probably a silk loom; and a definite Crompton & Knowles loom with patent dates 1888 to July 30, 1897. The first two have no maker's name visible but are thought to be Crompton & Knowles. Manufactured in 1897 or later, as the Crompton Loom Works and the Knowles Loom Works merged in 1897 to form Crompton & Knowles Loom Works. 15.5 x 10 cm.
