Biographical / Historical

The Brotherhood of Railroad Signalmen [BRS] is a labor union in the United States. It was founded in 1901 and represents workers who are tasked with maintaining railroad signal systems, highway-rail crossing warning systems and fabricating railroad signal equipment. The BRS is affiliated with the AFL-CIO and is a member of the Transportation Trades Department.
Inclusive date range: circa 1949
Bulk dates: circa 1949
This collection consist of four black and white photographs. On is identified as the General Chairman's Meeting in Chicago, Illinois at the Morrison Hotel on September 24-25, 1949. The second, which has a Toledo, Ohio photographer's stamp on the back, is a picture of 13 unidentified men. The third photograph shows 11 unidentified men. At least one particular man appears in each of these photographs, but he is unidentified. The final picture is a candid shot of men and women at a formal dinner.
