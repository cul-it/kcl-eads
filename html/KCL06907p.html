Biographical / Historical

Lone Star was first founded as the El Paso Cotton Mills Company in 1923; in 1932 the company was reorganized as Lone Star Cotton Mills, Inc. The company ceased production after 1980.

Include aerial and ground-level views of the exterior of the Lone Star Cotton Mills plant in El Paso, Texas. Also includes interior views featuring drawing frames, ring spinning frames, warping machines, the winding room, and the weaving room. The photographs were taken circa 1945.

Cars parked in side yard are somewhat obscured by trees. Water tower in the right background; lots of open space in the foreground. Partial railroad tracks in the left foreground. Format: black and white photograph

Scope and Contents
Gelatin silver print. Image depicts an aerial view of the Lone Star Cotton Mills plant in El Paso, Texas. The company consists of an office building (upper left background), a two-story concrete building with a one-story attachment, and several other buildings. Water tower is in the right foreground. Railroad cars on tracks in the upper background. One-story houses visible on the right. See item 4 in this folder for another aerial view from a different angle.
