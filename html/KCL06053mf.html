Biographical / Historical

The International Harvester Company (IHC, later IH) is now called Navistar International Corporation, was an United States agricultural machinery, construction equipment, vehicle, commercial truck, and household and commercial products manufacturer. J.P. Morgan merged McCormick Harvesting Machine Company and Deering Harvester Company in 1902, along with three smaller companies, to form International Harvester. International Harvester sold off its agricultural division in 1985 and renamed itself Navistar International Corporation in 1986. [Wikipedia]
Duplicate negative copies available upon request, housed in 6399 mf, box 7.
Access to the collections in the Kheel Center is restricted. Please contact a reference archivist for access to these materials.
