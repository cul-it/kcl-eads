Biographical / Historical

Company was founded in 1825 by Robert Bacon to make hats. John H. Bacon and Charles N. Bacon later joined the company. Twenty-five years later it began manufacturing all-wool sheet felt for the wallpaper trade, marble polishing and other mechanical uses. It also made felt wheels for polishing and burnishing. The company was bought by Stanley & Samuel McNeily in 1941.
Contains incoming correspondence, 1869-1870, 1873, concerning both business and family matters. Also includes a newspaper clipping from the Winchester Star, Oct. 25, 1979, giving a history of the company.

Processing Information

The ATHM # references the accession number given to collections by the American Textile History Museum (ATHM). These numbers have been kept and tracked for researchers looking for former citations. For images, these numbers will also be the digital object identifer if the item was scanned by the ATHM. The ATHM accession number for this collection was 0022.430. When these numbers appear in the processing note, the box and folder are the numbers that immediately follow the accession number. For instance, the number 0022.430.1.3, would indicate that the material was formerly in Box 1, Folder 3.
