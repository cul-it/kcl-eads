Biographical / Historical

Ralph Campbell (March 7, 1910 July 25, 1971) was professor of industrial and labor relations at Cornell University's School of Industrial and Labor Relations.
A graduate of Rutgers University (BA, 1931), Campbell served in World War II before completing his MBA at the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration in 1947. In 1948, Campbell joined the faculty at Cornell's School of Industrial and Labor Relations, and in 1956, he joined the faculty of Cornell's School of Education.
Campbell served the university in many administrative capacities over the course of his career. He directed the ILR School's Extension Division from 1948 to 1956, and again from 1960 to 1963. From 1956 to 1958, he directed Cornell's Summer Sessions, and from 1958 to 1960, he led the ILR School's Department of Human Resources and Administration. Nationally, Campbell was active in the National Institute of Labor Education, National University Extension Association, American Arbitration Association, the Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service, among other organizations.
See the Cornell University Faculty Memorial Statement for Professor Ralph Campbell: http://ecommons.library.cornell.edu/handle/1813/18574
