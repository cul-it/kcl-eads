Biographical / Historical

George Meany (1894-1980) was elected president of the American Federation of Labor (A.F. of L.) in 1952. His efforts to unite his organization with its rival, the Congress of Industrial Organizations (CIO), was successful, and he was elected president of the merged organization, the AFL-CIO, in 1955. He was a vigorous anti-communist and an outspoken member of the Democratic Party, who, by virtue of his office, wielded considerable influence in the party.
Interview broadcast on CBS radio and television stations, conducted by George Herman and Bruce Morton (CBS News) and Frank Swoboda (BUSINESS WEEK).
The following personalities and issues figure in the interview: the Democratic Party, left-wing Democrats, Richard M. Nixon, Americans for Democratic Action (ADA), George S. McGovern, Edward M. Kennedy, Nelson A. Rockefeller, Arthur J. Goldberg, Vietnam, Meany's Labor Day message, strikes, the United Automobile Workers, civil service workers , railroads, import quotas, House Trade Bill/Mills Bill, and the United Mine Workers of America.
