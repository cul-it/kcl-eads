Biographical / Historical

Daniel Nilva was born to Russian Jewish immigrant parents, in New York City on March 7, 1914.
Nilva attended Cooper Union in the 1930's, though he never graduated he met some lifelong friends, Bernard Seaman, a labor cartoonist, Morris Stone, later an official with the American Arbitration Association, and Dr. Valentine Gregory Burtan, a heart specialist who had been imprisoned on a conterfeiting charge.
In 1933 he started working as a photographer for the Education Department of Local 22, Dressmakers, of the International Ladies' Garment Workers Union. He also was a photographer for the ILGWU newspaper 'Justice'. He did some freelance work for the Bookkeepers Stenographers and Accountants Union's newspaper, 'The Ledger'.
His job with Local 22 only lasted 4 years. By the 1950's, after having trouble finding the right position, he was working as a freelance photographer in New York City, many of his clients were labor unions and progressive organizations.
Nilva's health took a turn in the 1960's when he suffered a heart attack, so he had to cut back on the amount of work he could do.
Daniel Nilva passed away September 15, 1984 as a result of another heart attack.
Access to the collections in the Kheel Center is restricted. Please contact a reference archivist for access to these materials.
