This collection is arranged in four series: Proceedings, American Labor Party (ALP), Political Action Committees (PAC), and Miscellaneous Documents. Correspondents in Series III-IV include Sidney Hillman, Chairman of CIO-PAC, and Jacob Potofsky, Chair of the Fundraising Committee under CIO-PAC.
Access to the collections in the Kheel Center is restricted. Please contact a reference archivist for access to these materials.

Biographical / Historical

The American Labor Party (ALP) was established in 1936 by labor leaders in the AFL and CIO out of the Labor's Non-Partisan League and Social Democratic Federation. The CIO Political Action Committee (CIO-PAC) was established in July 1943 to support the 1944 re-election of President Franklin D. Roosevelt and to promote CIO-endorsed political candidates at the local and national level. The committee was chaired by Sidney Hillman, head of the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America (ACWA), from 1943 to 1946.
