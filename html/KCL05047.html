Biographical / Historical

On January 21, 1958, the president appointed an Emergency Board to hear the dispute between Eastern Air Lines and its Flight Engineers. The Board consisted of David L. Cole, chairman, Saul Wallen and Dudley E. Whiting. The same people wereappointed to hear the dispute between Eastern Air Lines and the Pilots (Emergency Board #121) since one of the main issues in dispute in both cases was about whether the flight engineer should come from the engineers or the pilots.
The Flight Engineers wanted a specific guarantee that Eastern Air Lines would use only Flight Engineers on its larger aircraft, which required a third man who was responsible for the mechanical functions of the operation of the aircraft. Inaddition the Engineers wanted Improved pensions, wages, vacation time, traveling expenses and some others.
