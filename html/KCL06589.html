Biographical / Historical

The company was founded in 1879 and was located in Stafford Springs, Connecticut, with selling offices on Madison Avenue in New York. It manufactured fine wool suitings and overcoatings. The company was sold in 1963 to the Stevens Companies. During the period covered in this collection, it operated 10 sets of cards, 100 looms, and 4,000 spindles. In 2014, The American Woolen Company bought the Cyril Johnson and Warren Mills in Stafford Springs, CT to produce their American made textiles.
Collection contains incoming correspondence, 1930-1963, on topics such as machinery, advertising, fashion, business conditions, imports and mill closings. It also contains cloth test results compared with Japanese cloth for tensile strength and shrinkage, including consulting report from Barnes Textile Associates; survey of carding machinery with recommendations from Davis & Furber Machine Co.; reports on cloth production for the armed forces, 1941-1945, and other war- related materials; papers concerning the mill closing, 1962-1963; and information files including clippings, pamphlets, notes and articles on wages and hours, company history, fashions, mill closings, and imports.
