Biographical / Historical

Mather & Platt of Manchester, England, was formed in 1853 by the merger of two machinery building companies, one begun by Pete Mather in 1817 and the other, Salford Iron Works, begun by John and Thomas Sherratt in 1829 and later taken over by John Platt. Mather & Platt made textile machinery, steam engines, turbine pumps, dynamos, and other machinery.
Includes images of steam engines and other machinery built by Mather & Pratt during the late 19th century. Includes images of a turbine pump, dynamos, a boring machine, diagonal steam engines, a printing machine, and other machinery.
