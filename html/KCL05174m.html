Biographical / Historical

The Wheeling & Lake Erie Railway Company [WLE] was a railroad that operated primarily in Ohio from 1871 to 1988. The railroad was designed to connect the coal fields around Wheeling, West Virginia with port cities on Lake Erie. In 1948, the WLE was leased to the Nickel Plate Road (New York, Chicago and St. Louis Railroad). In 1988, the WLE was merged into Norfolk and Western Railway.
This collection is comprised of five pamphlets from the Wheeling and Lake Erie Railway Company describing Work Agreements, Regulations, Wage Rates and Rules.
