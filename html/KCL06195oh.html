Description
Date
Box 1 Folder 1
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, May 24, 1984. Part 1
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
5/24/1984. Run time 0:47:45. Topics include Leon Stein discussing the Taft Hartley Act impact on the union, David Dubinsky let the union lawyer act independently, Dubinsky's announcement in the 1960's that some people in the union were being untruthful and his response, Dubinsky plan to stay healthy, the health club at Unity House, meetings at Unity House, Arthur Goldberg's relationship with Dubinsky, Eleanor Roosevelt at Unity House after her husband passed away, Dubinsky's ability to relate to and work with a wide range of people, Dubinsky's 1933 National Recovery Act radio address, Dubinsky's relationships with President Harry S. Truman and President Dwight D. Eisenhower.
Box 1 Folder 2
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, May 24, 1984. Part 2
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
5/24/1984. Run time 0:47:53. Topics include Leon Stein discussing his warm relationship with John F. Kennedy, office staff organization and efficiency, the I960 election and relationship with Lyndon Johnson, the union's support of Johnson's second term, ILGWU officials travel to Washington, DC to be present for Martin Luther King Jr.'s speech, Dubinsky's support for King's movement, Johnson's support for the union, Johnson's close relationship with Dubinsky, Dubinsky advised him on matters of labor, Dubinsky's relationship with Robert Kennedy, John V. Lindsay, Alex Rose, Dubinsky's knowledge of the union rank and file, and Stein's editorials for Justice.
Box 2 Folder 1
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, June 5, 1984. Part 3
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
6/5/1984. Run time 0:47:19. Topics include Leon Stein discussing his experiences with David Dubinsky, his rise, presidency, and service to the ILGWU, Stein's opinion of why Dubinsky chose to retire from ILGWU, articles about Dubinsky's effectiveness before he retired, and his retirement, Paul Jacobs, cartoons and articles about Dubinsky and the Communists, Harry Haskel, turmoil and the Civil Rights movement of the 1960's, Charles Zimmerman, Unity House, organizing, and speeches, Dubinsky's leadership style, use of the concepts of social work and case studies to help rank and file members.
Box 2 Folder 2
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, June 5, 1984. Part 4
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
6/5/1984. Run time 0:46:39. Topics include Leon Stein discussing the announcement of Dubinsky's retirement, Louis Stulberg's national level contributions and presidency, reactions to Stulber's presidency, others who might have been qualified and the reason Stulberg was chosen, growth of the union, public relations between the union and rank and file, news articles, reactions and impact on the union, and Unity House.
Box 3 Folder 1
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, June 5, 1984. Part 5
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
6/5/1984. Run time 0:47:21. Tape begins mid-interview. Topics include Leon Stein discussing union mergers, 'out of town' - 'in town' issues, references to the union rank and file to reflect the function and needs of each sector, Dubinsky's response to ethnic differences in the rank and file, Dubinsky, Sidney Hillman, and the Russians, Dubinsky's local focus and Hillman's national focus, political participation by the union in gubernatorial and presidential races and conventions, Dubinsky's leadership style, organizing history of the ILGWU, attaining membership in the union, meeting David Dubinsky for the first time, the Garment Cutter publication as a forum for opposition to the union, Dubinsky's management of 'nativists,' 'old timers,' vs. other ethnic groups wants and needs during contract conflicts, Stein's oral history with Dubinsky on overcoming nativists and Communists, Dubinsky's office set up, publisher requesting a meeting with Dubinsky, and photographers coming to the office.
Box 4 Folder 1
Leon Stein oral history interview by Robert Parmet, June 5, 1984. Part 6
 1984
Format: Audio cassette tape
Scope and Contents
6/5/1984. Run time 0:47:39. Topics include Leon Stein discussing a trip to Fall River, Louis Nelson, David Dubinsky, and Stein, union expansion, Dubinsky's friendliness ability to fit in with any group of people, difficult negotiations with some employers, the impact of Dubinsky's accent slowing conversations momentarily, his love of all arts, the ILGWU Labor Stage which became the ILGWU Clubhouse, the 1958 general strike, 1962 legislation to correct conditions of the Triangle Fire, employee benefits, pension and retirement fund management, mortgages and military housing, Dubinsky's bargaining style.

 1984
