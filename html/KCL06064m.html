This collection consists of assorted documents and memorabilia of Sidney and Bessie Hillman. Included are a telegram, memorandum, printed material, immigration documents, and materials relating to the construction of the Sidney Hillman Mausoleum.
Access to the collections in the Kheel Center is restricted. Please contact a reference archivist for access to these materials.

Biographical / Historical

Phil Fried (Philoine Fried), 1917-2019, was the daughter of two labor leaders, Bessie Abramowitz and Sydney Hillman, and supported her parents' work in many ways throughout her lifetime. She worked for decades with the Jewish Labor Committee, and is a founding member of the New York Labor History Association, where she served as a treasurer until only a couple of years before her 100th birthday.
Bessie Abramowitz Hillman (born Bas Sheva Abramowitz, May 15, 1889 - December 23, 1970) was a labor activist and founder of the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America. She led the 1910 Chicago Garment Workers' Strike, which brought about the creation of the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America labor union in 1914.
